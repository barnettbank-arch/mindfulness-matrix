Breath Library

Simple, repeatable breath patterns to pair with your meditations. Use them as pre-ritual warm-ups, nervous system resets, or quick breaks between tasks.

Box Breath (4 · 4 · 4 · 4)

Inhale for 4, hold for 4, exhale for 4, hold for 4. Repeat for 1–3 minutes. This pattern is balancing and often used to steady the system.

  • Best for: steadiness, pre-meeting calm, mental clarity.
  • Try pairing with: forest or sky visuals.

5-5 Calm Breath

Inhale slowly for 5, hold gently, exhale for 5. This is the pacing Mira uses in many Mindfulness Matrix meditations.

  • Best for: easing anxiety, down-shifting after a long day.
  • Try pairing with: any guided meditation on the channel.

Moon Rituals & Intention Setting

The moon moves in cycles, and many people use the new moon to plant intentions and the full moon to release what they're ready to let go of. You don't have to believe in anything mystical to benefit — the ritual itself is a way of pausing, reflecting, and choosing what you want to invite into your life.

This is a powerful window for setting intentions, journaling, and visualizing the life you're growing toward.

You can keep things simple:

  • Light a white candle for clarity, or a green candle for growth/abundance.
  • Take a few slow breaths to settle your body.
  • Write 3–5 intentions in the present tense, as if they're already unfolding.
  • Read them out loud slowly, with gratitude.

How to word intentions

Intentions work best when they are specific, kind, and present-tense. You're not begging the universe; you're partnering with it.

  • Instead of: "I don't want to be anxious anymore."
    Try: "I am learning to feel safe slowing down, one breath at a time."
  • Instead of: "Please send me money."
    Try: "I am opening to aligned opportunities that support me financially and creatively."
  • Instead of: "I'm scared of being alone."
    Try: "I am calling in relationships that feel mutual, healthy, and nourishing."

You can close by saying something like: "Thank you for the ways this is already shifting, even if I can't see it yet."
