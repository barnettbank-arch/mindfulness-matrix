Moon Rituals & Intention Setting

Loading…
Countdown to the next new moon – a powerful window for intentions.

Use the new moon to set intentions, and the full moon to release what you’re ready to let go of. You don’t have to believe in anything mystical to benefit from it – the ritual is a way of pausing, reflecting, and choosing what you want to invite into your life.

New Moon Ritual Basics

  • Light a white candle for clarity or a green candle for growth and abundance.
  • Take 5–10 slow breaths to settle your body.
  • Write 3–5 intentions in the present tense, as if they’re already unfolding.
  • Read them aloud slowly, with gratitude.

Full Moon Release

  • Use a darker candle (deep blue, purple, or black).
  • Write down what you’re ready to release (patterns, stories, habits).
  • Say: "I release this with love. Thank you for what it taught me."
  • Optional: safely burn or tear the paper as a symbolic release.

Crystals & Sage for Calm

Think of crystals and cleansing practices as physical anchors for your intentions. They don’t have to be mystical to be meaningful: they’re tools to help your nervous system associate certain objects, scents, and rituals with safety and presence.

Core calming crystals

  • Amethyst – soothing, sleep-friendly, supports overactive minds.
  • Rose Quartz – softens self-talk, heart-healing, gentle self-compassion.
  • Clear Quartz – “amplifier” stone, good for setting clear intentions.
  • Black Tourmaline – grounding, protective, helps with feeling scattered.
  • Green Aventurine – growth, optimism, small courage for trying again.

How to work with them

  • Hold a stone in your hand while you meditate or breathe.
  • Place one by your bed as a small “calm anchor” before sleep.
  • Keep a pocket stone for stressful moments (calls, meetings, travel).
  • When you pick it up, mentally repeat your intention (e.g., “I am safe enough to slow down.”).

Sage, smoke & cleansing

Many people use smoke (sage, palo santo, incense) to reset the mood in a room. Be mindful and respectful: some plants like white sage are culturally significant to Indigenous communities and are often overharvested.

  • Choose ethically sourced herbs or local alternatives (rosemary, lavender, cedar).
  • Open a window so smoke can actually carry things out.
  • Move slowly around the room, breathing gently, not rushing.
  • Repeat a phrase like: “Anything heavy or stale is welcome to leave now.”

Non-smoke alternatives

You don’t have to use smoke at all if it doesn’t feel good to your body or lungs.

  • Sound – chimes, singing bowls, even a favorite song.
  • Water – a cleansing shower with intention, or placing stones in moon water.
  • Air – opening windows, letting fresh air move through.
  • Light – candles, soft lamps, or simply turning harsh lights down.

The point isn’t the object — it’s the state it helps you enter.

These 22 cards represent big themes and turning points – the “major chapters” of transformation.

  • The Wanderer (The Fool) – new path, trust, beginner’s heart.
  • The Weaver (Magician) – creative power, focused intention.

How to read your card

Treat every card as an invitation, not a verdict. A simple way to read:

  • Notice – What word or image in the card pops out first?
  • Feel – What does your body do when you see it (tighten, soften, lean in)?
  • Connect – Where does this theme show up in your life right now?
  • Respond – What tiny, realistic action could honor this message today?

You can always write your own meaning. Tarot is a conversation between symbol, body, and lived experience.

Suit cheat sheet

  • Roots – Earth: body, home, money, stability.
  • Rivers – Water: emotions, intuition, relationships.
  • Branches – Fire: energy, passion, creativity.
  • Stone – Air: thoughts, communication, boundaries.

When you pull a suit card, ask: “In this area of life, what is this card asking me to see?”
